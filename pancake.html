Crispy Kimchi Pancake Recipe

4.9 from 25 reviews
  • Prep time: 5 min
  • Cook time: 20 min
  • Yield: 4
Print Recipe
Ingredients
  • 2 & 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 1/2 cup water
  • 1/2 tsp sea salt
  • 1 large egg, beaten
  • 2 cups kimchi
  • 1 tbsp kimchi liquid
  • 5 ice cubes
  • 2 green chili
  • Sliced green onion, for topping
  • 1 red chili
  • Some vegetable oil for frying
Nutrition Facts
Serves 4
  • Total Fat : 8g
  • Cholesterol : 0mg
  • Calories: 318

Description

A kimchi pancake is one of the easiest and quickest things to make and is one of Koreans’ all time favorite dishes. It’s hot, spicy, crispy, a little sweet, sour, and a little salty. It is not only a great snack but also it goes well with rice as a side dish.

Instructions

  • 01. Make the pancake batter per the following. Sieve the flour and the salt. Add the water and whisk it well. Add the beaten egg, Kimchi, Kimchi liquid and chilies. Mix them well. Add the ice cubes to keep the batter cold.
  • 02. Pre heat a pan / skillet until well heated. Put a generous amount of cooking oil into the pan. Make sure the oil is spread all the way over the pan.
  • 03. Scoop out the pancake mixture with a ladle, pour it out on the pan. Make sure it is evenly spread.
  • 04. Cook the mixture on high heat initially for 10 to 20 seconds then reduce the heat to medium to low. (Cook and finish with this temperature setting.)
  • 05. Turn the pancake over when 70% of the pancake is cooked. (Per the above picture – Meaning the bottom of the pancake is cooked and the top of the pancake is also partially cooked. This makes it easy to turn the pancake. ) Press the pancake with the spatula a couple of times to make it crispy.
  • 06. When both sides are cooked, slice it into easy to bite-size. Serve with Korean dipping sauce (soy sauce, rice vinegar, sugar, chili flakes, or anything you want to add.)

Notes

i

If you have Korean pancake mix, which is pre-seasoned, you can use it instead of flour. In this case, omit the salt.

i

I made 4 large Kimchi pancakes with the ingredients above, but you can make more but smaller sized pancakes if you prefer. (Turning the pancakes will be easier if the pancake size is smaller.)

Korean cuisine is largely based on rice, vegetables, and meats. Traditional Korean meals are named for the number of side dishes that accompany steam-cooked short-grain rice. Kimchi is served at nearly every meal. Commonly used ingredients include sesame oil, denjang, soy sauce, salt, garlic, ginger, gochukaru, gochujang, and napa cabbage.
