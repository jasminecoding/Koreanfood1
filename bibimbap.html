Bibimbap Recipe

4.5 from 25 reviews
  • Prep time: 5 min
  • Cook time: 20 min
  • Yield: 1
Print Recipe
Ingredients
  • 2 cups medium-grain Korean rice
  • 1 large cucumber (sliced into thin strips)
  • 1 1/2 cups bean sprouts
  • 1 1/2 cups spinach
  • Dash of sesame seeds
  • 2 carrots (julienned)
  • 4 shiitake mushrooms (sliced)
  • 1 zucchini (sliced into thin strips)
  • 2 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1/2 pound meat
  • Fried egg
  • Gochujang
Nutrition Facts
Serves 3
  • Total Fat : 8g
  • Cholesterol : 0mg
  • Calories: 525

Description

Bibimbap is a popular Korean dish of rice topped with fresh and cooked vegetables and a red chili paste called gochujang. Seasoned raw or grilled beef may also be added, and a fried egg is often eaten on top.

Instructions

  • 01. Gather the ingredients.
  • 02. To begin, you'll need to cook the Korean rice in a rice cooker or on the stove.
  • 03. Next, give the cucumber strips a saltwater bath for 20 minutes. Drain the cucumber strips using a sieve.
  • 04. Then, season spinach with 2 teaspoons sesame oil, 1 teaspoon salt, and a dash of sesame seeds.
  • 05. Season bean sprouts with 2 teaspoons sesame oil, 1 teaspoon salt, and a dash of sesame seeds.
  • 06. You'll then need to sauté the carrots with a dash of salt.
  • 07. Next, sauté the mushrooms with a dash of salt.
  • 08. After that, sauté the zucchini with a dash of salt.
  • 09. Then, place the cooked rice in a large bowl and arrange vegetables on top.
  • 10. If desired, beef, egg or both can be placed in the center.
  • 11. Serve each helping with small bowls of gochugang and sesame oil.
  • 12. To eat, add a small amount of oil and desired amount of gochujang to your bowl and mix everything together with a spoon.

Notes

i

This recipe includes six vegetables, but if that's too many for your liking, you can instead make it with just three or four veggies or with whatever you happen to have in your fridge.

i

Koreans usually eat this rice dish with some beef, but if you're not a fan of red meat, don't despair.

Korean cuisine is largely based on rice, vegetables, and meats. Traditional Korean meals are named for the number of side dishes that accompany steam-cooked short-grain rice. Kimchi is served at nearly every meal. Commonly used ingredients include sesame oil, denjang, soy sauce, salt, garlic, ginger, gochukaru, gochujang, and napa cabbage.
