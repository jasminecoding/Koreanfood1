Tteokbokki Recipe

4.9 from 25 reviews
  • Prep time: 5 min
  • Cook time: 20 min
  • Yield: 1
Print Recipe Pin Recipe
Ingredients
  • 1 pound tteokbokki tteok (about 24 3-inch long rice cake pieces)
  • 1 sheet eomuk (fish cake)
  • 4 ounces green cabbage
  • 1-2 scallions
  • 3 cups anchovy broth or water
  • 3 tablespoons gochujang
  • 1-3 teaspoons Korean red chili flakes
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon corn syrup
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic
Nutrition Facts
Serves 3
  • Total Fat : 4g
  • Cholesterol : 0mg
  • Calories: 225

Description

Tteokbokki, spicy stir-fried rice cakes, is a highly popular Korean street food and a delicious comfort food you can easily make at home. The spicy, slightly sweet, and chewy rice cakes are simply addictive!

Instructions

  • 01. Soak the rice cakes for about 20 minutes if hardened or refrigerated. Cut the fish cake, cabbage, and scallions into about 2-inch long pieces.
  • 02. Add the anchovy broth (or water) to a large pan. Stir in the sauce ingredients. Bring it to a boil over medium high heat, stirring to dissolve the red chili pepper paste (gochujang).
  • 03. Add the rice cakes. Boil until the rice cakes become very soft and the sauce is thickened, about 8 - 10 minutes. Stir frequently so the rice cakes don't stick to the bottom of the pan.
  • 04. Add the vegetables and fish cakes. Continue to boil, stirring constantly, for an additional 4 - 6 minutes. Depending on rice cakes, you may need more time to reach a desired level of softness. Add more broth or water as necessary. Taste the sauce, and adjust the seasoning if needed. Serve immediately.

Notes

i

You can find tteokbokki rice cakes fresh, refrigerated, or frozen at Korean markets. Locally made fresh rice cakes are the best ones to use, but good quality refrigerated ones are okay too. Try avoiding frozen ones if you have other options.

i

Tteokbokki doesn't reheat well in the microwave. Reheat any leftovers, along with a little bit of broth or water, in a small pan over low heat.
