Yakgwa Recipe

4.3 from 50 reviews
  • Prep time: 40 min
  • Soak time: 3 hours
  • Cook time: 15 min
  • Yield: 20 cookies
Print Recipe
Ingredients
  • 3 cups flour
  • 1/4 cup sesame oil
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1/4 cup sake
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 3 cups veg oil for frying
  • 1/4 cup pine nuts
  • 1 tbsp toasted sesame seeds
  • 1/2 cup rice malt syrup
  • 1 cup honey
  • 1 piece ginger
  • 1/2 cup water
Nutrition Facts
Serves : 20
  • Total Fat : 20g
  • Cholesterol : 0mg
  • Calories: 445

Description

Yakgwa is a traditional Korean sweet, and it's usually shaped into some sort of flower design. It's deep-fried and very sweet, so it's more of a dessert than an everyday cookie, and it was traditionally served at ceremonies, celebrations, and on special occasions.

Instructions

  • 01. Place flour in a large bowl. Add sesame oil and mix together with your hands. Rub the flour between your hands and fingers to combine.
  • 02. In another bowl, whisk honey, sake, and water together. Add to flour mixture and knead gently with your hands to form a dough.
  • 03. Wrap dough in plastic wrap and set aside for 30 minutes.
  • 04. After 30 minutes, on a floured surface, roll out dough to half an inch thick.
  • 05. Cut dough into one-inch strips to make diamond or rectangle shapes. Or cut into a flower shape.
  • 06. Put a small hole in the center of each cookie.
  • 07. To make syrup, put rice malt syrup, honey, and ginger in a saucepan over medium heat. Bring to a simmer and then remove immediately from heat.
  • 08. Pour into a rectangular dish or glass baking pan.
  • 09. Bring a sturdy, flat-bottomed fryer or saucepan with oil, making sure the oil doesn't go higher than half the height of the pot. Heat over medium heat until oil temperature is 212 F.
  • 10. In small batches, drop pastries in oil and fry, gently turning them until they puff and float (about 4 to 5 minutes).
  • 11. Now raise the heat of the oil to about 300 F and continue to fry until the yakgwa turns a golden brown. Remove yakgwa from oil and place into the dish with ginger syrup.
  • 12. When all of them have been placed in the syrup, turn each one over once so they are coated. Soak in the syrup for a few hours, and then remove with a slotted spoon onto another dish topped with parchment.
  • 13. Sprinkle with pine nuts and sesame seeds.

Notes

i

Yakgwa can be stored at cool room temperature for several days or can be kept in the fridge for longer periods. It can even ben frozen for even longer storage. Adjust amount of liquid by adding a bit more soju if you feel the dough is too dry.
